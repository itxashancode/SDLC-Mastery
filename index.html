Waterfall Methodology

The traditional, linear approach to software development with sequential phases.

Waterfall Process

Waterfall follows a strict sequential flow where each phase must be completed before the next begins. This methodology is known for its structured approach and comprehensive documentation.

Phases

6 sequential phases

Documentation

Extensive & detailed

When to Use Waterfall

Well-defined Requirements

When project requirements are clear, stable, and unlikely to change

Regulatory Compliance

For industries requiring extensive documentation (banking, healthcare)

Fixed Constraints

When budget, timeline, and scope are predetermined and cannot change

Agile Methodology

An iterative, flexible approach that embraces change and delivers value continuously.

Agile Framework

Agile breaks development into small iterations (sprints) where each iteration delivers working software. This allows for continuous feedback, adaptation, and early value delivery.

Sprint 1 Sprint 4 of 5
Principles

12 Agile principles

Frameworks

When to Use Agile

Dynamic Requirements

When project requirements are expected to evolve or are unclear

Customer Collaboration

When continuous customer feedback is essential for success

Rapid Delivery

When quick time-to-market provides competitive advantage

Methodology Comparison

Detailed comparison between Waterfall and Agile methodologies

Comparison Aspect Waterfall Agile Recommendation
Approach Linear & Sequential Iterative & Incremental Choose based on project stability
Requirements Fixed at beginning Evolve throughout Waterfall for stable, Agile for dynamic
Customer Involvement Beginning & end only Continuous throughout Agile for customer collaboration
Testing Phase After implementation Continuous with development Agile for early defect detection
Documentation Extensive & detailed Minimal, just enough Waterfall for regulatory needs
Flexibility Low - changes difficult High - embraces change Agile for uncertain projects
Risk Management Late discovery Continuous discovery Agile for high-risk projects
Project Size Small to medium Any size Waterfall for simpler projects

Choose Waterfall When:

Requirements are well-defined and stable

No significant changes expected during development

Regulatory compliance required

Industries like banking, healthcare, or government

Fixed constraints

Budget, timeline, and scope cannot change

Choose Agile When:

Requirements are unclear or evolving

Expect frequent changes based on feedback

Need early ROI

Quick time-to-market provides competitive advantage

High customer involvement needed

Continuous feedback shapes product development

Hybrid Approach: Water-Agile-Fall

Many organizations adopt a hybrid approach that combines elements of both methodologies, often called "Water-Agile-Fall" or "Wagile". This approach allows for upfront planning while maintaining flexibility during execution.
